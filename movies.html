The Top 10 Movies Of March 2025

Below we have compiled a list of the top ten movies of March 2025.

The list is numbered from 10-1, saving the best for last.

ENJOY!

10. Den of Thieves 2: Pantera

This action-packed sequel to the 2018 heist thriller dives deeper into the intense rivalry between the elite LA County Sheriff’s Department and a highly skilled crew of bank robbers planning a massive heist across international borders.

  • Starring: Gerard Butler, O’Shea Jackson Jr., and 50 Cent.
  • Suitable For: Fans of action-packed thrillers with intricate heist plots and tactical combat sequences.
  • Where to watch: HBO Max, Apple TV, Google Movies.

9. Conclave

Set within the secretive halls of the Vatican, Conclave is a political thriller that follows the tense election process of a new pope. As the cardinals gather, power struggles, hidden alliances, and buried secrets come to light, revealing the darker side of religious politics.

  • Starring:Ralph Fiennes, Stanley Tucci, and John Lithgow.
  • Suitable For: Those interested in political dramas, historical intrigue, and suspenseful storytelling.
  • Where to watch: Available in theathers , Apple tv, and Amazon Prime

8. The Electric state

In a visually stunning retro-futuristic setting, a teenage girl embarks on an epic journey across the American West, accompanied by a mysterious yet endearing robot and an eccentric drifter. As she searches for her lost brother, she uncovers hidden secrets about the dystopian world she inhabits.

  • Starring:Millie Bobby Brown, Chris Pratt, Ke Huy Quan.
  • Suitable For: Sci-fi enthusiasts who enjoy emotionally driven adventures with breathtaking visuals.
  • Where to watch: Available on Netflix.

7. A Complete Unknown

This biographical drama chronicles the rise of legendary folk musician Bob Dylan, capturing his transformative years in the 1960s music scene. Timothée Chalamet delivers a mesmerizing performance, embodying Dylan’s enigmatic persona and artistic genius.

  • Starring:Timothée Chalamet, Elle Fanning, and Edward Norton.
  • Suitable For: Music lovers, fans of Bob Dylan, and those who appreciate well-crafted biographical films..
  • Where to watch: Available on Theathers, hulu and rental platforms.

6. Companion

Companion is a sci-fi thriller that follows a couple’s weekend trip with friends that spirals into chaos when it’s revealed that one of them is a robot. Packed with twists and ethical dilemmas, the story explores control, identity, and autonomy in a tech-driven world. With sharp writing and suspenseful storytelling, it keeps viewers on edge until the end.

  • Starring: Sophie Thatcher, Jack Quaid, Lukas Gage, Megan Suri, Rupert Friend.
  • Suitable For: Teens and adults who enjoy sci-fi thrillers with psychological depth.
  • Where to watch: Google Movies, Apple TV, Prime Video.

5. Dog Man

Based on Dav Pilkey’s bestselling graphic novel, Dog Man is an animated comedy-adventure about a half-dog, half-human police hero created after a freak accident. As he battles the mischievous Petey the Cat and other quirky villains, Dog Man learns about friendship, responsibility, and redemption. Full of laughs and heartfelt moments, it's a perfect watch for families and fans of the original books.

  • Starring: Pete Davidson, Lil Rel Howery, Isla Fisher, Ricky Gervais.
  • Suitable For: All ages, especially kids and fans of the Captain Underpants universe.
  • Where to watch: In Theathers.

4. One of Them Days

One of Them Days is a hilarious and chaotic buddy comedy about two best friends scrambling to find rent money after their roommate blows it all. As wild schemes unfold—from stealing sneakers to throwing an art gallery—the film explores friendship, hustle, and second chances. Packed with energy, humor, and heart, it stars Keke Palmer and SZA in her film debut.

  • Starring: Keke Palmer, SZA, Katt Williams, Patrick Cage, Maude Apatow.
  • Suitable For: Teens and adults who enjoy fast-paced comedies with strong female leads.
  • Where to watch: Apple TV, Prime Video, YouTube, Google Movies.

3. Mickey 17

Mickey 17 is a mind-bending sci-fi black comedy set on a frozen alien planet, where a man named Mickey serves as an "Expendable"—a cloneable worker who dies repeatedly for dangerous missions. When one version of Mickey survives and returns to the colony, he must face off against his aggressive replacement while uncovering secrets about the native alien species and the corrupt leadership. Thought-provoking and darkly funny, it’s directed by Bong Joon Ho and stars Robert Pattinson in dual roles.

  • Starring: Robert Pattinson, Naomi Ackie, Steven Yeun, Toni Collette, Mark Ruffalo.
  • Suitable For: Sci-fi fans and viewers who enjoy dark humor and deep moral dilemmas.
  • Where to watch: Google Movies, Apple TV, Prime Video.

2. Novocaine

Novocaine is a darkly funny action comedy about Nathan Caine, a shy bank employee who can’t feel pain. After his co-worker—and crush—is taken hostage during a Christmas Eve robbery, Nathan launches a wild, injury-filled mission to save her. With unexpected twists, explosive action, and quirky humor, this high-energy film blends heart with chaos.

  • Starring: Jack Quaid, Amber Midthunder, Ray Nicholson, Jacob Batalon, Betty Gabriel.
  • Suitable For: Adults and fans of quirky, fast-paced action comedies.
  • Where to watch: Google Movies, Apple TV, Prime Video.

1. Anora

Anora is a critically acclaimed drama-comedy that follows Ani, a young sex worker from Manhattan who unexpectedly marries the son of a wealthy Russian oligarch. As her new life spirals into chaos, Ani faces manipulation, betrayal, and power struggles in a tense, emotional journey. Directed by Sean Baker, the film won the Palme d’Or at Cannes and multiple Oscars for its raw, character-driven storytelling.

  • Starring: Mikey Madison, Mark Eidelštein, Juri Borisov, Karren Karagulian, Vatše Tovmasjan.
  • Suitable For: Mature audiences interested in emotionally intense, award-winning cinema.
  • Where to watch: Google Movies, Apple TV, Prime Video.
